Mexican food

While you could eat at a Taco Bell or Del Taco while in San Diego, please don't. You're better than that. We're better than that. For some seriously cheap, authentic mexican eats, look for any of the many many little fast-food walk up huts called "--bertos" or "--nitas" (Roberto's, Aliberto's, Juanita's, etc) and order something. Anything. Unless you have dietary restrictions, it doesn't really matter what everything is - but if you need to know, you can just ask the guy who takes your order. My personal faves are carne asada or machaca for a burrito; probably carnitas or al pastor for a taco. Don't be expecting any of this 'gourmet' burrito bullshit, either - if it's good, it's just marinated grilled meat, salsa, maybe some beans and guacamole. All that other crap you get from Chipotle is just pointless tasteless filler.

Unless you're from a big city in Canada, you probably haven't had a fish taco and the idea may even scare you a little bit. That's okay! It scares everyone, at first. If you want to ease into it, find yourself a Rubio's and order a fish taco especial. If that doesn't convince you, stick with the carne asada - we won't judge. If you love it and want to try more, OR if you've been trained to think that all fish tacos are battered and deep fried beasts, head down to Ocean Beach, to South Beach Bar & Grill. Depending on the season, you can get grilled mahi, shrimp, octopus, or even langostina (slipper lobster) in a super fresh tortilla with loads of spicy salsa and crunchy cabbage. As a bonus, they usually have an amazing selection of local beers on tap, and if you get there before the crowds, you can sit at the bar with a view out towards the beach and the OB pier.
